The expanded vision

Today is tomorrow's history.

curIosities was born from old attics and forgotten drawers. But history isn't only made in the past. Every ordinary moment happening right now is an artefact in waiting — and the people living it are the only ones who can preserve it.

A fireman photographs his crew — Blue Watch, 2026. Today it's just a team photo. In fifty years, it's a historical document. Someone's grandchild searches for it. A documentary maker finds it. A historian studying emergency services in the 2020s unlocks it. And the people in that photo — or their families — get paid every single time.

Nobody thinks of themselves as a historian while they're living their life. But they are. The nurse photographing her ward team on the last day of a hospital being demolished. The factory worker documenting the final shift before a plant closes. The schoolteacher uploading her class photo every September. The regiment photographed before deployment. The pub landlord capturing his regulars on closing night. The kid photographing a town centre before the developers move in.

All of them are making history. curIosities makes sure they own it — and profit from it — forever.
